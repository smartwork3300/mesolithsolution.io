4 Important Traits That Every Successful Recruiter Must Have

Tony Sorensen, CEO of VersiqueSearch and Consulting and McKinley Consulting, one of the leading Minnesota IT and business consulting services group, says “recruiting is a competitive industry, full of people who love to celebrate the win, so one of the most important traits of a recruiter is their competitive drive”. Sorensen further adds, “It’s an attitude that reflects the desire to help the client hire top talent and the candidate land a great new position”. The next important trait for being a successful recruiter is the ability to nurture relationships and maintain meaningful connections. Although some recruiters try to connect with as many people as possible on a superficial level, to create a vast network; it is actually the deep connections that allow successful recruiters to unify candidates and clients who suit each other. In this connection, Sorensen says that “Your reputation as a recruiter and your business will be much better off by building genuine relationships and maintaining them over time.” Further, soft skills like confidence, goal-orientation, listening, relationship building, communication, time management and marketing the company and the role are also important traits of a successful recruiter, says Farhan Farooqui, President and CEO of HR Milieu, a Dallas, Texas-based HR consulting service provider. Farooqui further added that a successful recruiter must be a team-player, having phenomenal problem-solving skills and should be technology-driven. Apart from these, Farooqui pointed out four crucial traits that are essential for the success of any recruiter. Read the blog below to know about the important traits that every successful recruiter must have.
1. Good understanding of the business and leading styles
Apart from having a good relationship with the hiring managers, a recruiter also needs to have a good understanding of the business and where the candidates shall fit. Not only this, a recruiter needs to have a good communication skill to be able to provide a realistic picture of the position and the leadership team or management with which the candidate needs to work while in job.
2. A strong technical aptitude and understanding
A recruiter needs to analyze the candidate from multiple angles. Those days are now a long past when Facebook and other uses of internet were considered a taboo in a workplace. Now-a-days a recruiter not just need to look at what the candidate tells about himself/herself on the resume, but also at what is not been said. Being technically savvy is crucial these days. According to Farooqui, “Searching Google is a common practice for successful recruiters”. Adding further, “Being able to see the Tweets, Facebook posts, and LinkedIn presence of the candidates is becoming a necessity. Many times, what the resume says is quite different from what the same candidate said on LinkedIn or another social media site. Remember, not everyone has their tweets protected”.
3. Having a futuristic approach
Farooqui says, to many recruiters, candidates are their next “fill”. Although filling a job is important, but on-the-job success of the candidates is crucial to any recruiting process. Once on-boarded the new recruits are forgotten by many recruiters. This is the key to maintain relations with the old recruits. Such individuals not only are a source of finding more talent to the recruiter, but a quick phone call to a new recruit after 90 days of working with the new company goes a long way. Farooqui further adds that, “We like to call a candidate after 90 days and ask a simple question – ‘Is this the position what I described to you during the interview process?’ If the answer is no, there is something to learn for the future hires”.
4. Orientation towards customer service
Finally, both Farooqui and HR Milieu put great stress on candidate’s/applicant’s experience. Farooqui believes in providing world class experience to all those who are interested to work in any position. In this connection, he says, “Hired or not hired, the recruiter should pay extreme attention to each applicant and feel that successful recruiting includes reputation management, which originates from candidate experience. It’s not what the new hire says – but what do the “non-selected” candidates say about your company, your professionalism, the process, and if asked, will they apply for another position at your company?” Thus, recruiting is tough, but successful recruiters know to make it through.

Candidate Relationship Management and Its Impact on Your Hiring Strategy

Just like every other industry, the process of identifying and acquiring skilled workers to meet organizational needs has also witnessed a drastic change over time. All the innovations, starting from help wanted signs, to job boards, to career sites, to AI technology and virtual assistants, are made with the intention of making it easier for the employers to blazon their jobs and for the job seekers to discover and grab the opportunities.
However, in spite of these advances, it has sometimes been felt that the hiring cycle has become more transactional than it ever was. Since the organizations channel candidates through various stages of technology and sometimes various layers of red tape, it is no wonder that the job seekers can sometimes be left pondering whether they really know the organization at all or where do they stand in the hiring process. In addition to this, with the current employment trends, the candidates these days look towards a company’s values, try to have a better understanding of whether the job and the organization is fit to them and most importantly, a more intuitive connection with the hiring team. In order to overcome these challenges, more and more employers these days are seen to opt Candidate Relationship Management (CRM) as an addition to their present tech stack.
CRM And Its Importance
Similar to various other hiring strategies, the concept of Candidate Relationship Management for CRM also started in the consumer world. Knowingly or unknowingly everyone of us have been the recipient of numerous relationship marketing campaigns. The ongoing branded messages in the form of loyalty programs, custom product recommendations on the basis of past purchases, and request for feedbacks for services rendered, etc. that we receive in our inbox are of a kind. It has been seen that; these marketing methods are frequently used by the brands that we interact with. This is because the ROI is very clear: the consumers who engage with these channels are more likely to make repeated purchases, give referrals and most importantly, have a positive insight towards these companies that make use of these kinds of communication tactics.
The good thing about this kind of campaign is that they are very successful when applied to candidate communications. For example, messaging about active opportunities, initiatives, and company value proposition. The relevancy and the core purpose behind these sorts of communications can bring about higher candidate engagement. It has been further observed that, candidates are more interested in receiving communications with relevant information that can lead them to a life changing new job or career.
Adding CRM to hiring strategies, the hiring team can find solutions to multiple hiring challenges. Candidate relationship can be helpful to those who:
Want to offer a recruiter a method to surface relevant candidates from diverse Are interested in tapping into an owned and interested group of talented job seekers Are looking to automate their communications and workflows Need an easy way to connect with the candidates before application CRM the much-needed platform that can serve all these purposes.
Ways To Successfully Deploy CRM
Although every organization’s way of deploying Candidate Relationship Management is based on their own needs and current processes and likely to be different from others, yet there are certain things that need to be kept in mind to ensure the successful deployment of CRM.
At the the very first instance it is important to consider how relationship marketing shall align with your organization’s overall strategy and how a CRM platform, in particular, can function as a channel for the organization’s broader initiatives. Think about the the team’s final goals. Is it to increase the headcount, or hit on a targeted diversity goal, or decrease the spending on media, or support a new business unit? Whatever may be the objective, the relationship marketing can function as a strong vehicle to drive results.
After identifying the goals, you can begin the process of drafting your communication plan. First of all, define your audience by deciding who you will be targeting and what kind of content you want to deliver. The next step is to review how you are connecting to the audience at present, and then identify where would relationship marketing fit in this workflow. Once identified, you can decide on what your messaging structure shall look like and the behavioral triggers that you might like to utilize for any multi-step drip campaign. Finally, invest time in analyzing your results. After a campaign is deployed, review your engagement as well as your internal processes and consider where improvements are required for your next send.
Using Data to Charge Your CRM Strategy
The CRM is is undoubtedly a useful tool to add in your team’s toolkit. However, this form of communication can be augmented by the use of collected candidate data points. A CRM connected to a candidate’s behavioral, contextual, and explicitly shared data can provide greater customized experiences. As for example, say the physical location, time of visit, content viewed, source, and other on-site behavior of a candidate are captured and stored; this data can then be paired with even more data on candidates’ unique profile such as work experience, education, contact information, and more. This compiled data can then be made available to the CRM platform and can be leveraged in infinite ways, such as, delivering triggered email campaigns based on user’s interest and behavior on-site. This data can also be used to facilitate more targeted communication by a recruiter.
Which Platform to Choose?
It is the platform that you choose to bring strategy and data together is what differentiates a well-structured CRM plan. There are several options available, starting from ATSs with CRM-like functionality to consumer-based software, like Salesforce or MailChimp. However, if you are serious about activating your talent community, then it advisable to invest in a technology that makes your processes seamless and intuitive for both- the candidates as well as your internal teams.

4 Ways of Providing Compelling Virtual Candidate Experience

According to a recent survey conducted by Talent Board, improving candidates’ experience has been cited by employers as their primary recruiting activity for the year 2021. The survey further shows, 98% of the employers believe that improving candidates’ experience can be helpful in enhancing and protecting their employer brand.
However, the biggest challenge being faced by employers is that in the past year, a remarkable number of employers had to shift from an in-person hiring process to a virtual one, to respond to the covid-19 crisis, and this shift has not been an easy one.
Recent researches have shown that a positive virtual candidate experience makes the candidates 38% more likely to accept a job. This has made it very important for the companies to plan and implement a propitious virtual candidate journey, starting from outreach and initial screening to interviewing and onboarding.
Want to know about ways of providing a compelling virtual candidate experience? Here are four foundations that can help you!
Make communication your priority One of the biggest mistakes committed by the employers is that they leave the candidates to wait and wonder. This is a grave mistake if you wish to provide a compelling virtual candidate experience. In a HR Technology survey, it was indicated that, 63% of candidates were dissatisfied with the communication from their employers. In addition to this, the survey showed that 1/3rd of job seekers regard ‘responsiveness’ as the most essential attribute, when it comes to candidate experience.
A prompt, clear and consistent communication is what exactly a candidate expects from a hiring team while interacting virtually and this is what makes all the difference. Further, reaching out to the candidates at every stage of recruitment develops a strong initial connection between the candidate and the employer. In addition to this, the employers that are seen to provide guidance and resources such as, tips for online interviewing, FAQ guides, and pre-interview technology check list are seen to make a more positive impression among the candidates.
Use the right technology to speed up the recruitment process A survey conducted by Yello, a talent acquisition platform, revealed that more than half of the employers report their time-to-hire as three weeks or more. But, 81% of the candidates hold that they expect a two-week process. If you to find a similar disparity is lurking in your recruitment process, it is high time to switch to advanced technologies such as, Artificial Intelligence (AI), automation, and enhanced analytics to streamline the virtual candidate experience and shorten their time-to-hire.
In addition, it has been seen that companies where the job application process takes longer than five minutes to complete, have experienced a high drop of rate, as high as 75%. Therefore, these advanced technologies can also be used in the initial application stage to ensure that the application process is fast and easy to complete. This shall increase the number of submissions and develop a far better first impression of the employer in the minds of the candidates. Further, 85-95% of the candidates use their mobile phones during the application process. Therefore, it is essential for the employees to look for tools that support a seamless search application process optimized for mobile phones.
Use survey tools to make hiring a two-way process It has generally been seen that employers conduct surveys to gather feedback from their candidates and hiring managers at the key stages of the recruitment process, to create a better virtual candidate hiring experience. However, HR Tech tools can be a better option for it can automate sending candidate experience surveys, such as:
Survey on Job application process: This kind of survey can include a short questionnaire to collect the inputs from the candidates on their experience of using the application platform. This would help in gathering information on how accurately the positions and responsibilities were described. Post-interview Survey: This survey basically provides an opportunity to ask both the candidates and the recruiting team about any necessary changes that needs to be incorporated to improve and enhance the recruiting process.
Survey on offer rejection/candidate withdrawal: This kind of survey shall basically focus on asking questions to the candidates on their likelihood of applying in future. Further, in case where any candidate rejects the job offer, this survey would help the employers gather a feedback about the candidate’s overall experience and employer brand.
Make proper use of social media and employer sites to create accurate expectations Employer branding plays a very important role in the recruitment process. But it plays an even more important role in a virtual experience, since it is the default format that the candidates use to interact with the company. In the survey conducted by HR Technologist, it was indicated that more than half of the candidates were willing to share their experiences through social media. Further, 69% of the candidates were found to have posted about their negative social media experience. It has also been found that negative reviews prevent candidates from applying for a position. Therefore, it is very important for the employers to leverage their digital and social media channels in order to facilitate candidate experience and at the same time boost their employer branding.

Jobs in Covid Era

How to acquire career opportunities in the post-Covid era?

Covid-19 has impacted every sphere of human life, the work life without being an exception. The post Covid-19 job market is no longer the same as the pre Covid-19 job market, and therefore, our endeavor to get a job in 2021 is no more the same as it once used to be.
There is indeed no doubt that Covid-19 has and will continue to create a raucous in the world of professions. At this part of the year, our priorities and our approach to work have changed, compared to that in the last year. But ever had a thought that even our approach to search a job today calls for changes?
All the strategies that worked well in finding a job in the pre-Covid era, now demands certain changes. The post-Covid era demands from us the determination and willingness to stand out of our traditional job searching approaches and adjust ourselves to the new trends. Those capable of standing out of their traditional job searching approach, are the only ones who can land on their next job and advance their career in this post-Covid era.
Below we present some very important tips that would help you to advance your career in the post-Covid era:
Be Positive
‘Your outlook towards your career’ is the game changer. In the post covid-19 era, it is the outlook or the mind-set of the jobseekers that has been playing the most important role in securing them a new career opportunity. Most people these days are seen to have a very negative outlook towards securing a job. They are already seen to have made up their minds that they won’t be able to secure a job due to Covid-19. Therefore, instead of quitting before starting even starting your job endeavor, what is suggested is to be more positive, determined and to try understand the new trends in the market to pitch oneself in the right manner at the right time.
Be Bold
The next very important tip for advancing your career in post-Covid era is “be bold’. You need to be bold enough to step into new territories. Search for industries having highest number of vacancies. Even if you are new to the industry do not hesitate to give it a try. As for example, the Health Care, Logistics, IT, and the infrastructure and professional industry these days are seen to offer tremendous job opportunities. Therefore, be bold enough to step out of your comfort zone and consider job opportunities from different industries.
Make Proper Use of Social Media
Anything on your social media profile that goes against your professional reputation or anything that can cause havoc to it, needs to be removed immediately. Make proper and positive use of your social media profile. Keep updating your LinkedIn profile. Follow industries and organizations you are interested in. Use proper skills and keywords. Post on LinkedIn from time-to-time. This will reveal your interest in the market and help aid your application.
Show Your Flexibility
Owing to the large number of changes that are taking place both in the organization(s) and in the wider professional world, flexibility or adaptability has become one of the top-rated skills that almost every organization wants to have in its employees. It is therefore suggested to demonstrate your flexibility both in your resume as well as your interview.
Develop Connections
This is only possible when you are energetic and proactive in your job endeavor. Instead of merely sending your resume and sitting at home hoping for the best to happen (the traditional approach), engage yourself in reaching out to the concerned employer (through social media). Following up the concerned hiring manager would ensure that you are at the top of their mind. In fact, developing connections has become more important than ever before to make your way and shine. Therefore, it is suggested to make concerted efforts to draw the attention of the employer towards yourself.
Sell Yourself
Not everyone knows the art of selling oneself. Neither does this ability come naturally. But the era we are living in demands the art of selling oneself. In order to sell yourself, the very first requirement is to know ‘what are your selling points?’ Try to find out and understand your unique strengths and how your strengths can help strengthen the job you are applying/interviewing for. Once you are sure about your strengths, portray them. Do not forget to share concrete proofs of all your achievements for each of your selling points.
Further, always remember that the first impression you can make in an organization, is in the interview. So don’t miss dressing up professionally while going for an interview. This will help you sell yourself in a better way.

6 Ways to find the right job by improving your social profile

A satisfactory job is not so easy to find. In the era of increasing competition, getting ‘the dream job’ has become very difficult. While applying for jobs, one might wonder, how one finds a right job? What makes the hiring mangers hire one over others?
Well, one probable answer to these questions is the ‘social profile of the candidate’. A good social profile is something that is very necessary for any candidate seeking job. In this digital era, social media profiles have become an important instrument for accessing the personality of an individual preliminarily, as well as to find out one’s overall experience and expertise. Hence, it is essential for the candidates seeking job to have a top-ranking social profile. Here, are a few tips for the job seekers to create a high-ranking social profile.
1. Profile picture matters
Undoubtedly, a picture, though small, is very important. The very first thing that a placement consultant would look at, while searching for candidates, is the ‘physical appearance’. It is therefore, suggestive to avoid pictures that make one look younger, or have some quirky background in it. A clear, recent picture in professional attire, shot in a clean background is the best thing that can be added to one’s social profile.
2. Background picture speaks a lot
The next thing that attracts the hiring managers is the ‘background picture’. It is therefore suggestive to select a background picture that either provides an insight about one’s current work profile, or skills and expertise, or one’s interests. Apart from this, small, crisp and interesting quotes reflecting one’s personality can act as an icing on the cake.
3. Let them know, you are open to new opportunities
Attention! Here, it is important to remember that, one is looking for ‘a new job opportunity’, and hence, mentioning the same in the profile is very important. Here, the job seeker can include the profile and location he/she is interested in. This would make it easy for the manager to understand the interest of the candidate.
4. Locate yourself
‘Location’ is the next important element that needs to be mentioned in the profile. This is because organisations, at times, look for candidates from specific locations. Here, it is also suggestive to candidates to mention clearly, whether or not they are open to location change. This small bit of information can be effective in saving the time and energy of both, managers as well as candidate.
5. Speak about your own self
Remember! It is candidate’s own profile. Therefore, he/she needs to ‘speak up about his/her own self’. In very brief and crisp words, the candidate needs to write what he/she does, what is his/her work interest, what he/she wants to do in future, his/her achievements, his/her contribution professionally. However, while doing so, the candidate must limit oneself and not exceed.
6. Acquaint them with your right skills
Last but not the least, comes ‘skills’. The best suggested way of adding skills to the profile is finding out the most searched keywords in the candidate’s respective field, and giving them a position in his/her own social profile. This will help the candidate secure top position in the candidate’s list. Here, the candidates can mention about the projects he/she has worked on, his/ her expertise, his/her creations (if any), about the benefits bought to an organisation by him/her, and such others. Here, it is again recommended to remain simple, crisp, clear, limited and to the point, so that anyone could easily understand the candidate and his/her work(s).
All the best in your job search!

Key Attributes of Your Employer That You Must Look Before Applying for A Job

What are the factors that you look for while applying for a company? Which factor(s) help you decide whether or not to apply for a company? Is it the salary? Or is it the benefits that the company has to offer to you? Or is it their remote working options that attract you towards them? However, if you wish to build up a strong career, it is necessary for you to look beyond these common attributes and focus on the strength of the company you are applying for, their market share, their brand reputation, profitability and such as others. All these attributes have a great say in building up your career. Conversation with an expert recruitment consultant can assist you steer your career options. Apart from these, there are a few other factors which are equally important but only a few people look for it while applying for a company.
Curious to know about it? Stay with us here and we will let you know the key attributes of your employer that you must look for before applying, which is generally missed out by most of the applicants.
Innovation
A company’s commitment to innovation is an important factor that requires consideration. Innovation refers to the ability to create or develop a new product. In case you work in business strategy or R&D, innovation shall be your obvious attraction. However, innovation should be a core concern for every applicant irrespective of their field of work.
You might be wondering why is it necessary for YOU to take into consideration the ‘innovation’ factor of the employer. Here’s why innovation is important.
Innovation Is What Predicts the Future of a Company
In the present times, technologies such as Artificial Intelligence (AI) and Internet of Things (IoT) have been accelerating the rate of change and disruption in almost all the industries. Even the strongest company of today may find itself in a weak position tomorrow, if it fails to champion innovation; and this in turn will have an impact on all the employees of the company.
The Benefits of Innovation Scatters Everywhere
Although you might not have a direct role to play in the innovation efforts of the company, yet it can be useful to you. Innovation can bring variety and interest to your work. As per example, if you are in a finance team, innovation can give you and your team a fresh challenge to forecast the revenues for the new product line. Therefore, the benefits of innovation are not restricted only to those directly involved with it, rather its benefits reach every person in the company.
Innovation is Not Merely the Creation or Development of New Product(S)
Every successful company acknowledges that innovation is not only about developing new product(s) or giving a new face to the product(s). They understand well that innovation is much beyond development teams and customers facing products and services. Innovation is rather an agent for improvement of the entire organization. It is an opportunity to change things for the better, which matters to every job, whether it is a development team, or marketing services, customer services or a HR.
Provides An Opportunity to Build Your Resume
To crack an interview being able to tell an alluring story about your experiences is something very critical. Stories about innovation play a very effective role in captivating the interviewers. It becomes easier to talk about the differences that you have personally created and the impact that you have brought into a business; when you are directly involved with an innovation, compared to being a simple spectator of what someone else has created.
A Strong Culture Can Only Be the Foundation of a Good Innovation
Generally, the cultural attributes such as, honesty, transparency, ambition, purposefulness, collegiate organizations, and the like; which are essential for building an environment for innovation are the same attributes that make an ideal workplace.
Commitment
Now that if you believe that innovation is an essential attribute that needs to be considered before applying for job in any company, it is essential for you to find out which company is in the truest sense committed to innovation. In the era when every company claims its commitment to innovation, it is essential to understand that the real proof of innovation lies on what a company does, rather than on what it says.
Here are a few signs of true commitment to innovation.
The Processes Are Crystalline
Keeping aside the romantic notion of innovation as a amalgamation of art and chaos, in business settings the processes supporting innovation are inevitable. Therefore, it is advisable to look for clear picture of how the ideas are prioritized, collected, tested and rolled out.
There Are No Ivory Towers
Although some companies may have certain roles dedicated to innovation as a result of which the innovation work can sometimes be physically or culturally separated from the rest of the organization. In spite of this, in an organization it should be everyone’s business to contribute his/ her own path to innovation.
Healthy Attitude Towards Failure
The attitude towards failure is another important sign. “Fail fast, fail often” is the mantra prevalent in the enterprises, but in reality, failure can be unhealthy in case the team pulls them off from taking risks, or loose enthusiasm to succeed, after facing failure. The most appropriate attitude towards failure is somewhere between the two extremes, that is, looking for some success, but at the same time, accepting the failure and taking it as a lesson and working on it to improve oneself.
